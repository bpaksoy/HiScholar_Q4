What is the ACT?

The ACT, also known as American College Testing, is a standardized college readiness assessment test for college admissions in the United States. The current version of the ACT takes 3 hours and 15 minutes to complete. Possible scores range from 1 to 36, measuring four 36-point sections - English, Math, Reading, and Science. The optional writing section is always administered at the end of the test and takes 30 minutes to complete.

SAT
Average SAT Score Range: 1365 - 1710

At Western State Colorado University, 20% of students submitted their SAT scores for college application and the average admitted student's SAT score was around 1537, which is slightly higher than the average score at other public institutions.

What is the SAT?

The SAT is a standardized test for most college admissions in the U.S. The main purpose of the test is to assess a student's readiness for college. The SAT takes 3 hours 45 minutes to complete. Possible scores range from 600 to 2400, measuring three 800-point sections - Critical Reading, Math, and Writing.

Note: Reported SAT scores are currently on the 2400 scale until until scores on the new 1600 scale are released in 2018.

Actual Tuition vs Inflation Adjusted Tuition

Price inflation causes the value of a dollar to fall over time, so the same dollar amount in two different years will usually represent different amounts of purchasing power. We've adjusted the historical dollar values to 2015's dollar value to account for inflation. Removing the effects of inflation from tuition allows you to see the true tuition changes, without external economic forces. For example, if a college increased their tuition by 5% over one year and the inflation for the year was 2%, the real rate of tuition increase is 3%.
